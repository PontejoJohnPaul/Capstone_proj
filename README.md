## A Solar-Powered IoT-Based Post-Harvest Monitoring System for Stored Palay with AI-Driven Predictive Pest Risk Alerts and SMS Notification for Local Farmers

### Members: 
- Pontejo, John Paul, S.
- Lumapaz, Kowein, C.
- Losa, Jhon Vincent, G.

### Purpose:
- To design and develop a solar-powered IoT-based post-harvest monitoring
system for stored palay (unmilled rice) integrated with AI-driven predictive pest risk
alerts and SMS notification functionality to reduce post-harvest losses and improve
storage management for local farmers.

### Scope:
- The scope of this study covers the design, development, and testing of an IoT based monitoring system intended only for local farmers palay (unmilled rice) storage environments. The system will collect and analyze environmental data such  as temperature, humidity, and moisture levels within the storage facility. These data  will be collected through IoT sensors and transmitted to the system for analysis. The  proposed system will be developed as a mobile application platform that allows  farmers to remotely monitor the storage conditions of their stored palay. The  application will display real-time sensor data and provide alerts when abnormal  environmental conditions are detected. The system will also integrate Application  Programming Interfaces (APIs) to enhance monitoring and prediction capabilities.  A weather API will be used to track the weather. This information will help provide  additional context in analyzing potential risks that may affect grain storage.
- The AI component will generate predictive insights and possible  recommendations or solutions to help farmers take preventive actions before  significant damage occurs. To ensure accessibility in areas with limited internet  connectivity, the system will include an SMS notification feature that will  automatically send alerts to farmers whenever abnormal environmental conditions detected.

### Definitions:
- Local Farmers (Nasugbu, Batangas) – Refers to the primary users of the system  who will monitor stored palay conditions and receive pest risk alerts and SMS  notification.
- Post-Harvest Losses (PHL) – Decline in grain quality of stored palay due to poor  storage conditions, pests, and environmental factors.
- Predictive Pest Risk Alerts – Alerts generated by the system to warn users about  possible infestation inside the storage based on analyzed environmental data.
- Risk Threshold – Predefined environmental limit used to classify safe, warning,  or danger conditions.

### System Architecture:

- The **System Architecture of the Solar-Powered IoT-Based Post-Harvest Monitoring System for Stored Palay with AI-Driven Predictive Pest Risk Alerts and SMS Notification** consists of five interconnected layers that enable real-time monitoring, data processing, predictive analysis, and notification delivery.
- The **IoT Monitoring Devices Layer** includes the Capacitive Moisture Sensor, DHT22 Temperature and Humidity Sensor, ESP32 Microcontroller, Solar Panel, and Rechargeable Battery. These components collect and process real-time environmental data such as temperature, humidity, and moisture levels.
- The **Data Transmission Layer** transfers sensor data through WiFi, internet services, and SMS gateway integration, ensuring real-time communication between the monitoring devices and the cloud server.
- The **Cloud/Processing Layer** manages data reception, storage, processing, and AI-driven predictive analysis. It integrates weather API data, maintains historical records, and generates pest risk alerts and SMS notifications.
- The **Application Layer** consists of the **Farmer Side** and **Admin Side**. Farmers can monitor storage conditions, manage batches, set thresholds, and receive alerts, while administrators oversee sensor monitoring, system logs, and device configuration.
- Finally, the **User Layer** represents the farmers and administrators who access the system through mobile or web applications. Together, these layers provide an efficient and reliable solution for monitoring and managing stored palay.
